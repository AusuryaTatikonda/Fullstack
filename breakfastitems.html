VADA

Ingredients:

  • salt
  • urad dal (split black gram)
  • green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • small piece of ginger, finely grated
  • Water
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Oil for deep frying

Recipe making

1.Wash the urad dal thoroughly in cold water and soak it in enough water for about 4-6 hours. Soaking makes the dal soft and easier to grind.
2.Drain the soaked urad dal completely. Do not use the soaking water. Grind the drained dal in a wet grinder or food processor. Add a little water as needed to make a smooth, thick batter. Avoid adding too much water; the batter should be thick, similar to a dough. Add salt to taste and mix well. The batter should have a thick pouring consistency.
3.Transfer the ground dal batter to a mixing bowl. Add finely chopped green chilies, grated ginger, cumin seeds, asafoetida, and salt to the batter.
4.Mix all the ingredients thoroughly to evenly distribute the spices throughout the batter.
5.Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium heat. Wet your hands with water to prevent sticking and take a small amount of batter in your hand. Shape it into a small ball and make a hole in the center with your thumb to form a doughnut shape. Alternatively, you can shape them into small flat patties.
6.Gently slide the shaped vadas into the hot oil. You can fry multiple vadas at once, depending on the size of your pan. Fry the vadas on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crispy. Flip them occasionally to ensure even frying.
7.Once the vadas are golden brown and crispy, use a slotted spoon to remove them from the hot oil. Drain the excess oil by placing the vadas on paper towels.

Serve Medu Vadas hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or any other chutney or dip of your choice....

DOSA

Ingredients:

  • rice (parboiled rice)
  • urad dal (split black gram)
  • idli rice (or use regular rice if unavailable)
  • fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
  • Water
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Equipment:

  1. dosa pan

Recipe making

1.Wash both types of rice and the dals separately under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds together in a large bowl with enough water for about 4-6 hours.
2.Drain the soaked ingredients well and grind them to a smooth batter using a wet grinder or a high-speed blender. Add water as needed to make a smooth batter. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter, neither too thick nor too thin.
3.Transfer the batter to a large container, add salt, and mix well. Cover the container with a lid or cloth and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight. During fermentation, the batter will rise and become slightly fluffy.
4.Heat the Dosa Pan,Grease the Pan,pour and spread the batter,Drizzle Oil,Cook Until Crispy,Fold or Roll then serve.

Serve dosas hot with your choice of chutney, sambar, or a potato masala filling (Masala Dosa).....

BONDA

Ingredients:

  • salt
  • urad dal (split black gram)
  • green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • small piece of ginger, finely grated
  • Water
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Oil for deep frying

Equipment:

  1. Idli steamer or pressure cooker with idli plates
  2. Idli molds (idli stand)

Recipe making

1.Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium heat. The oil should be hot but not smoking.
2.Wet your hands with water to prevent sticking and take a small amount of batter in your hand. Shape it into a small ball or patty. You can flatten it slightly with your fingers
3.Gently slide the shaped bondas into the hot oil. You can fry multiple bondas at once, depending on the size of your pan. Fry the bondas on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crispy. Flip them occasionally to ensure even frying.
4.Once the bondas are golden brown and crispy, use a slotted spoon to remove them from the hot oil. Drain the excess oil by placing the bondas on paper towels.
5.Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium heat.

Serve Urad Dal Bondas hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or any other chutney or dip of your choice.....

UPMA

Ingredients:

  • semolina (rava or sooji)
  • oil or ghee
  • mustard seeds
  • cumin seeds
  • finely chopped onions
  • finely chopped green bell pepper (capsicum)
  • finely chopped carrots
  • frozen green peas
  • green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
  • curry leaves
  • small piece of ginger, finely grated
  • Water
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • salt

Recipe making

1.Heat a heavy-bottomed pan or kadai over medium heat. Add the semolina (rava) to the dry pan and roast it while stirring constantly. Roast until it turns light golden brown and releases a nutty aroma. This should take about 4-5 minutes. Remove the roasted semolina from the pan and set it aside.
2.In the same pan, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter. Add the asafoetida, chopped green chilies, grated ginger, and curry leaves. Sauté for a minute until the spices become fragrant.
3.Add the chopped onions, bell pepper (if using), carrots, and green peas. Sauté the vegetables for 2-3 minutes until they begin to soften.
4.Carefully add 2 cups of water to the pan. Be cautious as it may sizzle. Season with salt to taste and bring the water to a boil.
5.Gradually add the roasted semolina to the boiling water while stirring continuously. This helps prevent lumps from forming. Reduce the heat to low and cover the pan with a lid.
6.Let the upma simmer for about 5-7 minutes on low heat. The semolina will absorb the water and cook completely. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking. If the upma becomes too dry, you can add a little hot water as needed.
7.Once the semolina is fully cooked and the upma is fluffy, turn off the heat. Squeeze some lemon juice (if desired) and garnish with chopped cilantro.

Serve Upma hot with coconut chutney, pickle, or a side of your choice.....
